Places To Visit

Malawi

Malawi, despite its modest size and landlocked location in southeastern Africa, attracts visitors with its beautiful lake, diversified wildlife, and pleasant people.

Tanzania

Tanzania is home to surreal natural wonders such as red-hued Lake Natron, the huge crater of Ngorongoro Conservation Area, and Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa's tallest mountain (and the largest free-standing mountain on Earth).

Botswana

Botswana has a strong conservation focus and offers numerous opportunities to see rhinos, elephants, and other wildlife in protected areas such as Khama Rhino Sanctuary, Chobe National Park, and Moremi Game Reserve, the Okavango Delta's oldest reserve.

Madagasca

Madagascar's habitat is unlike any other in the world: most of the country is untouched, allowing tourists to witness creatures in their native habitat. Visitors should keep an eye out for the country's mascot, the lemur.

Victoria Falls

Victoria Falls, located in Mosi Oa Tunya National Park on the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe, is about twice as deep and wide as Niagara Falls, making it one of the world's most spectacular waterfalls.

Nairobi

Kenya's capital city has the ideal balance of urban activities and natural beauty. Nairobi is a cultural hotspot, with museums exhibiting its pre- and post-colonial traditions, lively street markets, and a booming nightlife scene.

Namibia

Namibia, a country in southwest Africa, is known for the Namib Desert, which runs along the Atlantic Ocean coast. The country is home to a rich range of animals, including a sizable cheetah population.

Uganda

Uganda is a landlocked East African country with a diversified terrain that includes the snow-capped Rwenzori Mountains and the massive Lake Victoria. Its extensive biodiversity includes chimps and unusual birds.
